New Research Compares AI Governance Models Across Jurisdictions

EN Centre for the Governance of AI April 5, 2025
ai-safety research academic

A groundbreaking study from the Centre for the Governance of AI has compared regulatory approaches across 15 jurisdictions, finding significant variations in how countries are addressing AI governance challenges.

The research highlights that risk-based approaches are becoming dominant, though implementation details vary widely. The study also found that coordination between technical standards and regulatory frameworks remains a critical gap in most jurisdictions.

"Our analysis shows that while there's growing consensus on the need for AI governance, the specific mechanisms being deployed differ substantially," said lead researcher Dr. Emma Chen.

G20 Adopts Comprehensive AI Governance Framework

EN World Economic Forum April 3, 2025
policy international ai-principles

The G20 has unanimously adopted a comprehensive framework for AI governance, marking a significant milestone in international cooperation on AI regulation.

The framework establishes common principles for responsible AI development and creates mechanisms for cross-border collaboration on enforcement. It also includes provisions for regular assessment and updating of governance approaches as AI technology evolves.

"This agreement represents a breakthrough in how nations can work together to ensure AI benefits humanity while managing risks," said the G20 chair.

中国更新人工智能监管框架，强调伦理与安全

ZH Global Times April 2, 2025
regulation china ai-policy

中国国家网信办发布了更新版的人工智能监管框架，进一步强调了AI发展中的伦理考量和安全保障。

新框架要求所有大型AI模型在部署前进行严格的安全评估，并建立了更为完善的问责机制。该框架还特别关注了算法透明度和数据隐私保护，反映了中国在AI治理方面的最新思考。

"这一框架将确保中国的AI发展既能保持创新活力，又能符合伦理标准和安全要求，"一位政府官员表示。

日本のNGO、AIガバナンスに関する新ガイドラインを発表

ai-ethics japan ngo

日本の主要なNGO連合が、企業向けのAIガバナンスに関する包括的なガイドラインを発表しました。
